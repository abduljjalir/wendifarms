Waste Management Tips

Glass Disposal
🔄 Flip

Separate glass bottles and jars from other waste. Never break glass on the ground. Use safe containers to store glass before recycling. Proper disposal prevents accidents and environmental pollution.

Waste Bin Colors
🔄 Flip

Green bins for organic waste, red for hazardous, yellow for recyclables. Correct segregation ensures efficient recycling and safe disposal. Educate family members about bin usage to reduce contamination.

Plastics & Polythene
🔄 Flip

Separate plastic waste from wet and organic waste. Reuse when possible. Avoid burning plastics as it releases harmful chemicals. Proper recycling reduces environmental damage.

Paper & Cardboard
🔄 Flip

Recycle newspapers, office paper, and cardboard. Keep papers dry to avoid contamination. Proper disposal helps reduce deforestation and promotes a circular economy.

Waste Collection
🔄 Flip

Keep your bins accessible for collection vehicles. Segregate waste to help collectors easily process recyclables, organic, and hazardous waste. Timely collection reduces health risks.

Home Dust Bin
🔄 Flip

Maintain separate bins at home for wet, dry, and recyclable waste. Empty them regularly. Proper home segregation makes waste management easier and cleaner.

Organic Waste
🔄 Flip

Compost kitchen scraps and garden waste. Avoid mixing with plastics. Organic compost improves soil health and reduces landfill load.

Hazardous Waste
🔄 Flip

Batteries, chemicals, and medical waste should be handled separately. Never pour chemicals down drains. Safe disposal protects humans and environment.
